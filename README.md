## ğŸ” Key Churn Analysis Observations
- Senior citizens show a high churn rate, with **41.61%** leaving the service.  
- Gender does not significantly influence churn behavior.  
- Customers with shorter tenure are more likely to churn.  
- Month-to-month subscribers churn more than long-term contract customers.  
- Electronic check users have the highest churn rate.  
- Phone service users show higher churn in absolute numbers due to higher adoption.  
- Fiber optic internet users experience the highest churn among internet services.  
- Customers without online security, device protection, and tech support churn more.  
- Streaming TV and Streaming Movies have minimal impact on churn.  
- Paperless billing users churn more than non-paperless users.
